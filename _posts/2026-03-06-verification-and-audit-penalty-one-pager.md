*Published. Final.*

**Problem:** Anonymous and pseudonymous accounts enable foreign actors to impersonate Americans and bots to deliver propaganda at scale. Voluntary verification and detection are insufficient.

**Proposal:** (1) Require covered platforms to operate verification systems. (2) Mandate independent audits; require prominent publication of audit results. (3) Impose penalties that scale with fake-account count/rate, plus a campaign penalty when coordinated/state-run campaigns are found. (4) Three strikes (max penalty 3×) → suspension or placement under an independent entity until remedied.

---

**Verification duty**
- Platforms above a size/risk threshold must run verification (e.g., proof-of-personhood or “verified human”) so users can be “one person, one account” without disclosing identity.
- Standard: reasonable care—adequate design and operation, not perfection. Preserves anonymity while reducing bots and impersonation.

**Independent audit**
- Periodic (e.g., annual) audit by regulator or approved auditor: assess verification adequacy and estimate inauthentic-account count/rate using a statutory definition and standard methodology.
- “Inauthentic” = bot, impersonation, or coordinated inauthentic behavior (definition in law; exclusions for parody/satire).
- **Publish results prominently:** Platforms must publish each audit report in a prominent way (e.g., linked from homepage, in-app notice, dedicated transparency page)—not buried. Enables public and investor scrutiny.

**Penalty options**
- **Count-based:** $X per inauthentic account above a de minimis threshold.
- **Rate-based:** Escalating fines by % of MAU that is inauthentic (e.g., 2–5%, 5–10%, etc.).
- **Hybrid:** Base fine for inadequate verification + surcharge scaling with inauthentic count/rate. Cap at Z% of global revenue (e.g., DSA-style).

**Campaign penalty (on top of base)**
- If the audit finds a **coordinated or state-run influence campaign** that the platform should have detected and mitigated: add a **lump-sum or tiered fine** (e.g., coordinated campaign → $Y; state-run campaign → $Z), or a **multiplier** on the base fake-account penalty.
- Audit methodology must explicitly assess campaign presence (e.g., graph/behavioral analysis, threat-intel). Platforms pay for both overall inauthentic prevalence and for failing to prevent or remove serious campaigns.

**Escalation: three strikes (max penalty = serious violation)**
- Hitting the **maximum penalty** (cap) is treated as a serious violation. If a platform reaches the maximum penalty **three times** (e.g., three audit cycles): **suspend** the platform’s covered service (or relevant operations) **or** place it under an **independent entity** (receiver, trustee, or court-appointed monitor) until remediation is verified.
- “Remedied” = the entity certifies that verification, detection, and governance are fixed; a follow-up audit shows compliance; and the regulator restores normal operation. Design details (who appoints the entity, scope of suspension, appeal) to be set in law.

**Safeguards**
- Penalize only accounts a reasonable verification/detection system could have caught (or tie penalty to failure to verify + excess prevalence).
- Transparent audit methodology; appeal and judicial review.
- Avoid over-deletion: don’t reward platforms for purging legitimate anonymous users.

**Precedent**
- FTC: per-violation fines for fake reviews/influence ($51,744/violation). DSA: independent audits + up to 6% global turnover. X’s €120M DSA fine included misleading verification (blue checks).

**Ask:** Enact a verification duty, require independent audits with **prominent publication** of results, impose base + campaign penalties, and **escalate** to suspension or independent oversight (receiver/trustee/monitor) when a platform hits the maximum penalty three times, until remediation is verified.
